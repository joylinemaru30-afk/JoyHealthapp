Areas Affected by Typhoid

Major Regions Affected

Typhoid fever is most common in areas where clean water and proper sanitation are limited. The following regions are particularly affected:

  • South Asia: Countries such as India, Pakistan, Nepal, and Bangladesh report the highest number of typhoid cases.
  • Southeast Asia: Nations like Indonesia, Vietnam, and the Philippines often experience outbreaks due to unsafe water sources.
  • Sub-Saharan Africa: Many areas in Nigeria, Kenya, Ghana, and the Democratic Republic of Congo are affected because of inadequate sanitation facilities.
  • Parts of Latin America and the Caribbean: Some regions in Haiti and Peru still experience typhoid cases, especially where clean water is scarce.
  • Refugee Camps and Conflict Zones: Overcrowded conditions and lack of proper sanitation increase the risk of typhoid transmission.
